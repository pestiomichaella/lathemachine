## Overview

The tailstock sits on the right side of the lathe, opposite the headstock. Its main purpose is to support the far end of long workpieces to prevent bending and to hold tools for drilling and boring.

## Key Features

- **Quill**: Sliding spindle that holds centers or tools
- **Handwheel**: Manual feed control for the quill
- **Adjustable Position**: Can slide along the bed for different workpiece lengths
- **Locking Mechanism**: Secures tailstock in position

## Location

Positioned at the right end of the lathe, it provides support for the workpiece end and can be adjusted along the bed length.

## Function Details

### Primary Functions
- Support long workpieces to prevent deflection
- Hold centers for workpiece rotation
- Hold drill bits and reamers for hole-making operations
- Maintain precise alignment with the headstock

### Operating Process
1. Loosen the tailstock lock
2. Slide to appropriate position
3. Tighten the lock
4. Use handwheel to advance or retract the quill
5. Engage or disengage from the workpiece

## Maintenance

- Keep the quill clean and lubricated
- Check alignment with headstock
- Inspect lock mechanism for wear
- Ensure smooth handwheel operation
